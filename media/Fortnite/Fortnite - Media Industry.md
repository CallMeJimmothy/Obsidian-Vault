# Fortnite - Media Industry

## Social and Cultural Context

### Industry Growth
- Gaming industry has been **growing since the early days of Atari** (1980s entertainment)
- In recent years, **base entertainment** has moved to focusing on the **genres and platforms** games can be played on
- Based on **2015 economic forecast**: videogame sales are expected to reach **$90 billion by 2020**

### Market Size
- **2019**: 18 billion gamers worldwide = **25% of global population**
- Challenges stereotype of gamers as "young geeks"
- Men: 18-30% in their USA, 25% in gamers
- Women: under 18 but 23% were over 50 years old
- **Age diversity** in the gaming industry

## Epic Games as Institution

### Company Background
- Epic Games started by **Tim Sweeney** in his parents' bedroom at his parent's house
- **Humble beginnings** eventually led to Epic's decision to make the **Unreal Engine**
- **2012**: Epic Games was acquired and renamed **Unreal Engine** as the "most successful" videogame engine

### Development Strategy
- Epic Games owns video game developer **Chair Entertainment** and cloud-based software service **Cloudgine**
- Animation and VFX studios in the **UK, Japan, and Germany**
- **Tencent** (Chinese investment company) focused on internet and AI development
- Bought a **42% stake in Epic in 2012**

### Unreal Engine Success
- Licensed to AAA studios
- Used by **professional studios** like Epic Games
- Also used in **Batman: Arkham City** and **Infinity Blade**

## Funding and Business Models

### Games as a Service (GaaS)
- **GaaS model**: constant stream of content from the game purchases **after the initial purchase** (providing the game for free)
- Revenue sources:
  - **Micro transactions** where players can pay for extra volume
  - **Battle passes** with drip-feed exclusive content to players over the course of the season

### Revenue Success
- Fortnite franchise had **revenue of $2.4 billion in 2018**
- The franchise helped make **Epic Games a very successful company**
- **2012**: they were worth **$825 million**
- **2018**: they were worth **$5-8 billion**
- Epic Games CEO Tim Sweeney was named **PC Gamer** and **IGN awards in 2018**

### Alternative Revenue Streams
- **Micro transactions** where players pay for in-game items and upgrades rather than "grinding through" the gameplay
- These can be sold for **real money**, increasing value and encouraging players to pay rather than "grind"
- **Candy Crush** offers **upgrade packs** - "cosmetic" (doesn't affect gameplay)
- Other companies **host competitions** that allow access to new content over the course of an event (e.g., championship/tournament)

## Technology and Convergence

### Cross-Platform Play
- Key feature: **ability to access the game from consoles, PCs, laptops, smartphones or tablets**
- Can also download it for a **range of operating systems**
- "Not the first you can move, mid-game, **between devices without interruption gameplay**"
- Can be played **at home, on the move, on a tiny screen or a video projector**
- Example of **technological convergence**

### Media Brand Integration
- Epic Games has spent **relationship with Unreal Engine** to develop Fortnite
- Created site **unrealengine.com** for their website and **blogs** to reach fans on their own games
- **Unreal Engine** used by Hollywood and professionals to create trailers

### Cross-Media Presence
- More than one media brand or form present - **most viewed game on YouTube**
- Used influential platforms like **Twitch** for live gameplay
- Incorporated **other media brands and franchises** (e.g., collaboration with Marvel Studios - special *Avengers: Infinity War* segment released as DLC/in-game update)
- Any kind of costume, weapon, and dance can be **themed around real-world trends**
- Celebrities from **soccer, sports** can promote to over 20 million of players
- Athlete/musician collaborations keep Fortnite in the **news and social media**

### Celebrity Partnerships
- Epic Games has a **good relationship** with various non-gaming celebrities:
  - **Drake and basketball star Ben Simmons**
  - Helps to promote the game beyond traditional gaming market

## Age Ratings and Regulation

### Rating Systems
- Age ratings are systems used to ensure content is **appropriate**
- Computer games clearly labeled by age according to the content they contain
- Age ratings provide **guidance to consumers** (particularly parents) who have helped **decide what to buy**
- Rating confirms game is **suitable for players** of that age

### PEGI System
- **2012**: PEGI system incorporated into UK law via **The Video Standards Council**
- Statutory body responsible for the **age rating of video games** in the UK
- Fortnite has PEGI rating of **12** for "frequent scenes of mild violence"
- Seems that parents are **more concerned with issues surrounding addiction** than the levels of violence

## Genre Classification

### Gaming As Social Activity
- Gaming has increasingly become a **social activity**
- **1997 Ultima Online**: first on-line multiplayer game, socializing in virtual communities
- Allows for **millions of people**, usually geographically far apart
- **Forging friendships** and even **romantic relationships** as their game characters develop
- Competitive gaming events between celebrity players has also become popular
- Attracts **millions of viewers**
- Gaming events held in **large stadiums** like traditional sporting events

### Genre Characteristics
- In terms of **genre**, Fortnite could be considered as **Battle Royale** games (like *Battlegrounds* and *Counterstrike*)
- Games classified by **genre**:
  - From **first-person shooters to Battle Royale**
  - Vary in **demographics** for gamers
  - **Variety of platforms** and **modes of gameplay**
  - From **single-player to multiplayer**
- That high quality, **complex and engaging games can be accessed through** the smartphone
