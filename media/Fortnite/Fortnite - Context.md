# Fortnite - Context

← Back to [[Fortnite]]

## Historical Context

### Gaming Industry Evolution
- **1980s**: Early days of **Atari** - foundation of gaming industry
- Industry has been **growing** since these early days
- In recent years: focus shifted to **genres and platforms** games can be played on
- **2015 economic forecast**: Videogame sales expected to reach **$90 billion by 2020**

### Online Gaming Development
- **1997**: ***Ultima Online*** became first major online multiplayer game
- Pioneered **socializing in virtual communities**
- Allowed geographically distant players to connect
- Led to development of **friendships** and **romantic relationships** through gaming
- Established gaming as a **social activity**

### Epic Games History
- Started by **Tim Sweeney** in his parents' bedroom
- **Humble beginnings** → major success story
- Created the **Unreal Engine**
- **2012**: Renamed **Unreal Engine** as "most successful" videogame engine
- Company value growth:
  - **2012**: $825 million
  - **2018**: $5-8 billion

## Development Context

### Fortnite's Release
- **Released**: 2017
- **Developer**: Epic Games
- Built using their own **Unreal Engine**
- Series of **multiplayer free-to-play Battle Royale** games

### Product Context
- Available on **multiple platforms and devices**
- It was initially released for **Windows** and **macOS**
- Later expanded to **consoles** and **mobile devices**

## Social Context

### Changing Gamer Demographics
- **Old stereotype**: Gamers as "young geeks"
- **Reality**:
  - **Men**: 18-30% (USA), 25% overall
  - **Women**: Under 18, but 23% were over 50 years old
- Shows **age diversity** in gaming industry
- **2019**: 18 billion gamers = **25% of global population**

### Gaming as Social Activity
- Gaming increasingly becoming **social** rather than solitary
- Players forge connections through games
- Competitive gaming events attract **millions of viewers**
- Gaming events now held in **large stadiums** like traditional sports
- Celebrity involvement brings mainstream attention

## Economic Context

### Revenue Success
- **Fortnite franchise revenue**: $2.4 billion in 2018
- **July 2018 alone**: $1.2 billion
- Helped make Epic Games a **very successful company**
- CEO **Tim Sweeney** won **PC Gamer** and **IGN awards in 2018**

### Business Model Innovation
- **Free-to-play** model challenges traditional game sales
- **GaaS (Games as a Service)** model:
  - Constant stream of content
  - Revenue after initial (free) download
- **Micro transactions**: small purchases for cosmetic items
- **Battle passes**: seasonal content access
- Model encourages **ongoing spending** rather than one-time purchase

### Market Competition
- Part of **Battle Royale** genre boom
- Competes with games like ***Battlegrounds*** and ***Minecraft***
- Other games using similar models: ***Candy Crush*** (upgrade packs)

## Technological Context

### Cross-Platform Innovation
- Key innovation: **seamless play across devices**
- Can play on:
  - Consoles (PlayStation, Xbox, Nintendo Switch)
  - PCs and laptops
  - Smartphones and tablets
  - Various operating systems
- Can switch devices **mid-game without interruption**
- Example of **technological convergence**

### Unreal Engine Impact
- Epic's **relationship with Unreal Engine** enabled Fortnite development
- Engine licensed to **AAA studios**
- Used by **Hollywood professionals** for trailers
- Created site **unrealengine.com** for outreach
- Used in other major games: ***Batman: Arkham City***, ***Infinity Blade***

## Cultural Context

### Media Integration
- **Most viewed game on YouTube**
- Popular on **Twitch** for live streaming gameplay
- Collaborations with:
  - **Marvel Studios** (*Avengers: Infinity War* DLC)
  - **Musicians** and **athletes**
  - **Soccer/sports celebrities**
- Promotes to over **20 million players**
- Celebrity partnerships:
  - **Drake**
  - **Ben Simmons** (basketball)

### Real-World Trend Integration
- Costumes, weapons, and dances **themed around real-world trends**
- Keeps game relevant and culturally connected
- Appeals beyond traditional gaming market
- Keeps Fortnite in **news and social media**

## Regulatory Context

### Age Rating Evolution
- **2012**: **PEGI system** incorporated into UK law
- Enforced by **Video Standards Council**
- Statutory body for age rating of video games in UK

### Fortnite's Rating
- **PEGI 12**: "frequent scenes of mild violence"
- Relatively low rating despite combat focus
- **Parental concerns**: More worried about **addiction** than violence
- Debate about gaming's impact on young people

## Industry Context

### Epic Games Growth
- Owns video game developer **Chair Entertainment**
- Owns cloud-based software service **Cloudgine**
- Animation and VFX studios in:
  - **UK**
  - **Japan**
  - **Germany**

### Investment Context
- **Tencent** (Chinese investment company):
  - Focused on internet and AI development
  - Bought **42% stake in Epic in 2012**
- Shows global investment in gaming industry

### Competitive Gaming Growth
- Esports becoming mainstream
- Events held in large stadiums
- Millions of viewers
- Celebrity player involvement
- Similar to traditional sporting events
