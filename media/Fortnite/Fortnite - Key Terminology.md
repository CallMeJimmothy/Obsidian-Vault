# Fortnite - Key Terminology

← Back to [[Fortnite]]

## Business Models

### Games as a Service (GaaS)
- **Definition**: A business model where there is a constant stream of content from the game purchases after the initial purchase
- Provides the game **for free** initially
- Revenue comes from **ongoing purchases** within the game
- Examples: Battle passes, micro transactions

### Micro Transactions
- **Definition**: Small payments made within a game for additional content or features
- Players pay for **in-game items and upgrades** rather than "grinding through" gameplay
- Can be sold for **real money**
- Increases game value and encourages players to pay rather than "grind"
- In Fortnite: usually **cosmetic items** that don't affect gameplay

### Battle Pass
- **Definition**: A purchasable system that provides exclusive content over time
- **Drip-feeds** exclusive content to players over the course of a season
- Encourages repeated play
- Provides sense of progression and rewards

## Technical Terms

### Technological Convergence
- **Definition**: When different technologies, media, or platforms merge together
- Example: Ability to play the same game across **consoles, PCs, laptops, smartphones, or tablets**
- Can move mid-game **between devices without interrupting gameplay**
- Fortnite can be played **at home, on the move, on a tiny screen or video projector**

### Cross-Platform Play
- **Definition**: Ability to play a game on multiple different platforms/devices
- Players on different systems (PlayStation, Xbox, PC, mobile) can play together
- Removes barriers between gaming communities
- Key feature of modern online games

### Cross-Media Convergence
- **Definition**: When more than one media brand or form is present
- Example: Fortnite is the **most viewed game on YouTube**
- Also uses platforms like **Twitch** for live gameplay
- Incorporates **other media brands and franchises** (e.g., Marvel, musicians, athletes)

## Industry Terms

### AAA Studios
- **Definition**: Large, high-budget game development studios
- Produce major commercial games
- Example: Studios that license the **Unreal Engine**
- High production values and large teams

### Unreal Engine
- **Definition**: A game development engine created by Epic Games
- Licensed to **AAA studios** and professional developers
- Used in games like **Batman: Arkham City** and **Infinity Blade**
- Also used by **Hollywood** for trailers and VFX
- One of the most **successful videogame engines**

### DLC (Downloadable Content)
- **Definition**: Additional content released after the main game
- Can be free or paid
- Example: ***Avengers: Infinity War*** special segment in Fortnite
- Keeps game fresh and extends its lifespan

## Genre Terms

### Battle Royale
- **Definition**: A game genre where many players compete to be the last one standing
- Players dropped onto a map (often from the sky)
- Must scavenge for weapons and resources
- Play area shrinks over time, forcing conflict
- Examples: *Fortnite*, *Battlegrounds*, *Counterstrike*

### First-Person Shooter (FPS)
- **Definition**: Game genre where player sees through character's eyes
- Focuses on gun combat
- Different from Battle Royale but related

### Multiplayer
- **Definition**: Games designed for multiple players to play together
- Can be cooperative or competitive
- Online or local
- Opposite of single-player

## Audience Terms

### Demographics
- **Definition**: Statistical characteristics of a population
- Includes: age, gender, location, income
- Important for understanding game audiences
- Fortnite has diverse demographics: all ages, genders

### Virtual Communities
- **Definition**: Online groups of people who interact through games/platforms
- Players can forge **friendships** and **relationships**
- Example: **1997 Ultima Online** pioneered this
- Allows millions of people (geographically far apart) to socialize

### Esports
- **Definition**: Competitive video gaming at a professional level
- Events held in **large stadiums** like traditional sports
- Celebrity players attract **millions of viewers**
- Growing industry with sponsorships and prizes

## Regulatory Terms

### PEGI (Pan European Game Information)
- **Definition**: Age rating system for video games
- Ensures content is appropriate for age groups
- Provides **guidance to consumers** (especially parents)
- **2012**: Incorporated into UK law via **Video Standards Council**
- Fortnite has PEGI rating of **12** (frequent mild violence)

### Age Ratings
- **Definition**: Systems used to label content appropriateness by age
- Clearly labels games by the content they contain
- Helps parents decide what to buy
- Confirms game is **suitable for players** of that age
